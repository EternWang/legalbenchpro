# Application Positioning

This repository is meant to show research ability in AI-assisted social science,
institutional text analysis, and empirical legal/NLP evaluation.

## Core Strengths Demonstrated

### Research Engineering

The project turns a large, messy multimodel legal evaluation workbook into a documented
and reproducible research object. The public snapshot includes:

- a data card and release-status policy;
- sample extraction code;
- machine-readable metadata;
- a scoring and annotation protocol;
- a manuscript status file;
- a lightweight validation workflow.

### Legal And Chinese-Language Domain Knowledge

The Chinese real-case split shows the ability to work with de-identified legal
materials, issue framing, source constraints, and jurisdiction-specific reasoning
tasks. This is especially relevant for research involving Chinese institutional text,
legal records, policy documents, and high-stakes document interpretation.

### Evaluation Design

The benchmark separates scalable public-exam scoring from practice-oriented case
analysis. That design makes it possible to ask a more social-scientific evaluation
question: when does performance on convenient public benchmarks transfer to harder
institutional reasoning tasks, and when does it fail?

### AI-Native Reproducibility

The repo makes AI assistance explicit rather than invisible. It separates prompt
generation, model-output collection, scoring, human validation, and release review. This
is the workflow needed for credible AI-assisted research rather than one-off model
demonstrations.

## Short Pitch

LegalBenchPro is a reproducible benchmark project comparing LLM legal reasoning on
public legal-exam tasks and de-identified Chinese civil judgment prompts. The current
public snapshot includes a safe sample, dataset metadata, scoring protocols, manuscript
materials, and validation checks while full human validation and data-release review
remain in progress.

## How This Supports A Research Application

The strongest story to tell is not only that the dataset is large. It is that the
project shows a full research loop: define a substantive institutional-text problem,
curate structured data, evaluate multiple model configurations, build a scoring
protocol, expose limitations honestly, and make the workflow reproducible for other
researchers.
